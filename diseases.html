Diseases

Microorganisms such as bacteria, viruses, parasites, and fungus cause communicable (also known as infectious or transmissible) diseases, which can be transmitted directly or indirectly from one person to another. Some are spread by insect bites, while others are contracted by swallowing contaminated food or drink.

Chickenpox (Varicella)

The extremely contagious varicella zoster virus causes chickenpox. Coughing and sneezing, as well as direct touch with skin sores, transmit it.

Coronaviruses

Coronaviruses are a vast family of viruses that may cause everything from the common cold to more serious respiratory illnesses.

More Details

Dengue

The dengue virus is transmitted via the bite of an infected mosquito. Symptoms are moderate to severe that can be fatal in just a few hours.

More Details

Tetanus

Tetanus bacteria can be found in soil, dust, and manure, and can enter the body through skin breaks, such as cuts wounds created by contaminated things.

More Details

Flu (Influenza)

Influenza (flu) is a contagious viral infection of the nose, throat, and lungs that primarily affects people in the Philippines in June to November.

More Details

Measles

Measles is a highly contagious respiratory disease that can cause pneumonia, convulsions, brain damage, and even death.
