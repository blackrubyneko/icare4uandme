Chickenpox (Varicella)

The extremely contagious varicella zoster virus causes chickenpox. Coughing and sneezing, as well as direct touch with skin sores, transmit it.

Coronaviruses

Coronaviruses are a vast family of viruses that may cause everything from the common cold to more serious illnesses like Middle East Respiratory Syndrome (MERS), Severe Acute Respiratory Syndrome (SARS), and Novel Coronavirus (COVID-19). Coronaviruses can produce serious respiratory illnesses.

More Details

Dengue

The dengue virus is transmitted via the bite of an infected mosquito. Even if the symptoms are moderate, severe dengue can be fatal in just a few hours.

More Details

Tetanus

Tetanus is unique among vaccine-preventable diseases in that it does not transfer from one person to another. Bacteria can be found in soil, dust, and manure, and can enter the body through skin breaks, such as cuts or puncture wounds created by contaminated things.

More Details

Flu (Influenza)

Influenza (flu) is a contagious viral infection of the nose, throat, and lungs that primarily affects people in the Philippines in June to November.

More Details

Measles

Measles is a highly contagious respiratory disease that can cause pneumonia, convulsions, brain damage, and even death.
